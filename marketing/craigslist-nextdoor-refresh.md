## 1. CRAIGSLIST — 4 Fresh 2026 Ad Variants

> These are **new 2026 variants** — distinct copy, updated stats, and corrected credentials versus the four older templates in `docs/craigslist-ads.md`. Don't run both sets simultaneously in the same metro; they'll read as duplicates and get auto-flagged.

### Posting basics (applies to all 4)
- **Category:** Craigslist > **housing** > **real estate - by broker** (`reb`). For the Buyer Wanted ad, **real estate - by broker** is still correct (you are the broker seeking buyers). Do **not** post in "by owner" — that violates Craigslist rules for licensed agents and gets you flagged fast.
- **Region:** Nashville Craigslist (`nashville.craigslist.org`). Don't cross-post the identical ad to multiple regions on the same day — Craigslist's duplicate detector spans regions.
- **One live ad per variant at a time.** Running all four is fine because each has a distinct angle and headline.
- **Phone format:** write it as `615-551-2727` and also spell key words out; avoid stuffing the same phone number into 5 lines (triggers spam scoring).

### 48-hour repost rotation & avoiding flagging
1. **Delete-then-repost, don't "renew."** Every ~48 hours, delete the old post and create a fresh one. Craigslist ranks by recency, so a fresh post climbs back to the top.
2. **Rotate the four variants** rather than reposting the same one back-to-back. Sequence: Valuation → Cash Offer → Just Listed → Buyer Wanted → repeat.
3. **Vary the headline every repost.** Swap a word, the emoji, or the lead city (e.g., "Franklin" → "Brentwood" → "Spring Hill"). Identical titles are the #1 duplicate-flag trigger.
4. **Rewrite ~20% of the body** each cycle — reorder bullets, swap the opening line, change the example city/price. Boilerplate that's byte-identical gets ghosted (live to you, invisible to others).
5. **No URL shorteners, no all-caps walls, no repeated punctuation (!!!), minimal emojis.** These raise spam scores and earn community flags.
6. **Don't blast.** 1–2 posts/day max across categories. Posting the same offer in 6 categories at once = ghosting + IP throttling.
7. If a post gets flagged twice, **change the headline and the first paragraph substantially** before trying again, and slow your cadence.

## 2. NEXTDOOR

### Strategy note
Nextdoor's algorithm and its neighbors **reward genuinely local, helpful, conversational posts and aggressively penalize self-promotion.** Overt "list with me" ads get reported, hidden, or land you in the "Real Estate" classifieds where almost no one looks. The winning approach:

- **Lead with value, sign with a soft identifier.** Give a useful, specific, local update first. Your name + "Affiliate Broker, Compass" goes at the bottom as a sign-off, not a headline.
- **Post as a neighbor, not a billboard.** Use your real profile, write in first person, and reply to every comment like a conversation.
- **Cadence:** roughly 1 value post per 1–2 weeks. Daily promo = throttled reach + reports.
- **Use the right tools:** for anything that's clearly a listing or service, use Nextdoor's **business/real estate** posting tools or **sponsored** placement rather than dropping ads in the neighborhood feed. Keep the organic feed helpful-only.
- **No protected-class language, ever** — same Fair Housing rules as above.
- **Local proof beats bragging.** "Three homes on [Street] sold this spring" lands better than reciting awards. Mention credentials lightly and only where relevant.

## 3. COMMUNITY FACEBOOK GROUPS

### Which Middle TN groups to join
Prioritize active, locally-moderated groups. Read each group's pinned rules **before** posting — most ban overt agent self-promo and will remove you for it.

- **City & neighborhood community groups** — e.g., "[City] Community," "[Subdivision] Neighbors," "[City] Residents." The core audience.
- **Newcomer / relocation groups** — "Moving to Nashville / Middle TN," "New to [City]." High intent; people actively need housing help — but they're also moderated tightly against agent spam, so be the helpful expert, not the salesperson.
- **Buy / Sell / Trade & yard-sale groups** — good for *Just Listed* posts **only if the group allows real estate** (many don't — check rules; some have a dedicated weekly real-estate thread).
- **Local-interest groups** — "[City] Foodies," "Middle TN Moms" (general-interest, not protected-class targeting), school-zone parent groups, "What's Happening in [City]," local events/charity groups. Build presence and goodwill, not listings dumps.
- **Question/recommendation groups** — "[City] Recommendations," "Ask [City]." People literally ask "anyone know a good Realtor?" here — answer helpfully when it's organic, don't cold-pitch.

### Posting etiquette
- **Use your personal profile** (a Page often gets less reach and reads as an ad). Be a real, recognizable neighbor.
- **Lead with value every time.** Answer questions, share local info, be useful. Self-promo, if any, is the soft sign-off.
- **Follow group rules to the letter** — many require posts to go through an admin, restrict promo to a weekly thread, or ban it outright. Respect that or you're gone.
- **Engage, don't broadcast.** Comment helpfully on others' posts more than you post your own. Reputation compounds.
- **Don't spam-cross-post** the same text to 12 groups in an hour — admins talk, and Facebook throttles it.
- **Disclose you're an agent when relevant** (e.g., when recommending yourself or answering a Realtor request) — it's honest and it's often required.
- **Fair Housing applies here too.** No protected-class language in posts or comments.
