## How to use this calendar

**1. Batch it once a week, not daily.**
Block 60–90 minutes every Friday. Write/finalize the next 5–7 posts and queue them in the autoposter to its native days (GBP Tue, IG Wed, LinkedIn Thu, FB Mon/Wed/Fri). Shoot all your asset photos/graphics in one Canva session so you're never scrambling at post time.

**2. Repurpose one idea across every channel.**
Each "idea" here is a seed, not a single post. Example flow for one buyer-tip:
- **Instagram** → carousel (the version written above)
- **Facebook** → same copy, slightly longer, conversational
- **LinkedIn** → reframe to the professional/advisor angle
- **X** → cut to the single sharpest line + the link
- **GBP** → tighten to 1–2 sentences + a Call button
- **Reel/Short** → record yourself saying the carousel out loud (30–45 sec)
One concept = 5–6 posts. That's how 27 ideas covers far more than 27 slots.

**3. Hold the line on compliance every single time.**
Before anything posts: (a) Fair Housing gut-check — am I describing the *home* or implying who should live there? (b) Title reads "Affiliate Broker." (c) Any cash-offer mention keeps the **70–85% of ARV, as-is, no obligation** framing. (d) No invented stats, sales, or awards — `[PLACEHOLDER]` stays a placeholder until it's a real, approved fact.

**4. Track what actually moves leads — not vanity metrics.**
- Put a UTM on every link, e.g. `?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=buyer-tips`, so `/contact` form fills can be traced to a channel.
- Watch **saves + shares + DMs**, not just likes — those predict leads better than reach.
- Note which **pillar and which city** drives the most calls/texts to 615-551-2727 and form submissions, then shift next month's mix toward the winners.
- Re-run the top 2–3 performers next month with a fresh city or fresh asset. Winners repeat; you don't need a new idea every time.

**5. Facebook & Instagram are queued, not live.**
Those accounts are currently blocked. Keep producing this content on schedule and stage it in the autoposter so the backlog publishes immediately on restoration — don't let the gap create a content hole. GBP, LinkedIn, and X carry the load in the meantime.
