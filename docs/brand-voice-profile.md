## 2. Voice & style  *(review & adjust)*

- **Tone:** confident, warm, and **authoritative without being salesy**. A trusted local expert, not a hype guru. Never cheesy, never desperate.
- **Substance over fluff:** lead with specifics and real data (median price, days on market, actual neighborhood detail). Cut adjectives that aren't backed by a fact.
- **Local-first:** demonstrate on-the-ground knowledge (a specific street, builder, school zone, commute) rather than generic "great area" filler.
- **Plain language:** explain market concepts simply; no jargon dumps. Helpful, not condescending.
- **Benefit-forward CTA:** most pieces end with a clear next step — *"Call or text Joshua at 615-551-2727"* or a link to the relevant joshuafink.com page.
- **Honest & compliant:** no overstated guarantees; any "best/top" claim is backed by a real stat. Fair-housing safe — never describe *who* a neighborhood is "good for" in protected-class terms; describe the home, schools, and amenities.

## 3. Formatting preferences

- **Social posts (LinkedIn / Instagram / Facebook / X):** **entity-first** — start with "Joshua Fink Group…", not "We/Our/Just listed!". Put the target keyword + location in the **first 7–12 words** (that becomes the title tag). Write in **semantic triples** (entity → predicate → object): *"Joshua Fink Group just listed a home in Brentwood, TN…"*
- **Hashtags:** fine on Instagram / Facebook / X; **none on LinkedIn** (a hashtag hijacks the title tag away from the keywords).
- **Links:** point to the matching **joshuafink.com** page (not compass.com) so traffic lands on the owned site.
- **Length:** tight. Emails 3–6 short paragraphs; social captions scannable; listing descriptions vivid but skimmable.
- **Emoji:** sparingly and professionally (a leading 🏡/📍/💰 in a social post is fine; none in formal emails).
- **Dates:** ISO 8601 in any structured data (`2026-07-01`, not "July 2026").
- **Avoid:** buzzword filler ("nestled," "boasts," "must-see," "hidden gem"), ALL-CAPS shouting, and fake urgency.
