## 3. Recurring Cadence — Monthly Middle TN Market Update

**Why:** the 2025 market posts + may-2026 are the fastest-decaying assets. AI engines + Google reward recency on "market update" queries; a dated monthly post is the best recurring GEO magnet.

**Cadence:** one fresh Middle TN market update at the **start of each month** (Weeks 1, ~5, ~9 in this window can carry it — they're short/templated).

**Reusable template (fill brackets from a sourced report):**
- Median/avg sale price + **YoY %** — *cite Greater Nashville REALTORS® / RealTracs, with the month.*
- Active inventory + months of supply + **YoY %**.
- Days on market vs. prior year. % of list price received.
- One call-out each: Williamson, Davidson, Rutherford, Sumner/Wilson.
- "What this means if you're **buying** / **selling** this month" (2–3 sentences each).
- FAQ block: "Is it a buyer's or seller's market in Nashville right now?", "Are prices going up or down in Middle TN?", "What's the average home price in Williamson County?"
- Link `/market/[suburb]` + a buyer & seller CTA.

> ⚠️ **DATA IS HUMAN-INPUT, NOT AI-GENERATED.** Every statistic must come from a named, dated primary source (Greater Nashville REALTORS® / RealTracs, county assessor/trustee, THDA, Freddie Mac PMMS for rates). Draft prose with `[STAT — source, month]` placeholders; hold for Joshua/Chuck to drop in verified figures before publish. Same for property-tax rates (Wk 10) and THDA limits (Wk 5).

## 4. Content Guardrails

1. **No invented statistics, ever.** Any price/rate/tax/inventory figure must trace to a named, dated source, human-verified. When unsure, write qualitatively ("mid-6% range in mid-2026") and flag `[VERIFY]`.
2. **Cite sources inline** (link the primary source; date every data point).
3. **Match the house voice:** warm, plain-spoken, honest-about-tradeoffs, non-hype. Second person, calm, practical. No urgency-bait.
4. **Legal/tax/financial = educational only.** Reuse the existing disclosure pattern for foreclosure/taxes/capital-gains/probate and any post where Joshua may act as principal buyer (TREC #351484). Cash-offer/distress posts carry the principal-buyer disclosure + the "70–85% of after-repair value" honesty line.
5. **Genuinely useful, not thin.** Each post stands alone; CTA/links come after the value.
6. **Nothing Joshua hasn't approved:** no track-record claims, no client outcomes, no price/timeline guarantees. Route CTAs through existing `/api/contact` + city pages.
7. **GEO structure on every post:** lead with a 2–3 sentence extractable answer, question-shaped H2s, a 3–5 question `FAQPage` block, and ≥3 internal links to that week's money pages.
